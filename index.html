Practice Overview

Lanterns that move. Shadows that tell. Sounds that guide.
Inclusive, eco-conscious spaces for play, improvisation, and reflection.

Ann K. Chou is an artist-scientist and researcher working with lanterns, shadow animation, improvised sound, and access-led performance. Her practice explores how meaning shifts across light, movement, language, and sensory difference.

Grounded in lived experience as a hard-of-hearing Cantonese speaker, Chou develops adaptive methods rather than fixed representations. Lantern figures remain physically still while light moves, producing animated shadows that respond to proximity, pacing, and audience attention. Materials are chosen with care, often incorporating recycled or low-impact elements, supporting both accessibility and environmental awareness. Speech, captions, and bilingual wordplay are treated as evolving tools rather than complete systems.

Her work spans performance, installation, workshops, and research-creation, often unfolding through iterative public tests rather than finished objects. Across contexts, she is interested in how access can function as a creative method‚Äîshaping form, rhythm, and shared spaces for intergenerational encounter and creative care.

üñåÔ∏è Draw-Aloud Protocol in Action

In familiar, community-based settings, participants mapped their health journeys across three time landmarks‚Äîpast, present, and future‚Äîthrough live sketching and spoken reflection. These visual timelines became expressive, multimodal health records.

The Draw-Aloud protocol is a guided procedure designed to support emotional safety and narrative expression. It was facilitated either live in person or via virtual platforms. A self-directed version was also developed as a photo-mailback kit, enabling participants to engage independently while following a structured, reflective flow.

Each session or kit began with a grounding exercise to honor the emotional vulnerability of illness storytelling. To support interpretability by future healthcare providers, we adopted a ‚ÄúPatient Explanation Model‚Äù, allowing participants to annotate their drawings with short descriptions or keywords in their own words.

ü©∫ ePROMS Portal: Patient-Reported Symptom Interface

This interface explores how patients can report symptoms using clinically validated questionnaires. Implemented partially via LHC-Forms and custom FHIR JSON, it demonstrates how PROMs like PHQ-9 can be modeled and mapped to SNOMED CT and LOINC standards.

Status: Prototype ‚Äî not a functional portal, but ready for integration into Patient Health Record (PHR) systems that support JSON-formatted, standards-compliant PROMs.

Why it matters: Bridges patient voice and clinical structure, offering a design-led lens into participatory care tools and semantic interoperability.

Tools & Standards: HL7 FHIR, LHC-Forms, JSON, LOINC, SNOMED CT
